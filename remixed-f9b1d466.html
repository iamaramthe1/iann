What is RIR?
Reps in Reserve — a method for measuring training intensity.
Represents how many reps you could still do before reaching muscular failure.

Example: RIR = 2 → you did 10 reps but could have done 12.
Focus
Each day has a priority muscle group, but the whole body gets stimulated. Antagonists work in pairs — one muscle rests while the opposite is active.
